Ux Research & Ux design

RyanAir, a European low-cost company, was chosen for this project. This project aims at giving Inclusivity and Accessibility a higher position within the app by including Special Assistance directly into the ‘Book a flight’ flow.

Project Overview

This case study focused on improving accessibility within the RyanAir mobile app by integrating the Special Assistance feature directly into the 'Book a Flight' process. The goal was to make the booking journey more inclusive for:

  • Users with disabilities
  • Elderly passengers
  • Parents with young children
  • Travelers with specific health conditions

By embedding accessibility into the core user flow, the project aimed to reduce booking friction and improve the overall user experience.

Toolkit: Figma, Adobe CC, FigJam

Process

The project began with user research to understand the needs of travelers requiring assistance. The research phase involved:

  • Competitive Analysis: Studied both premium and budget airline apps to explore best practices.
  • User Interviews: Conducted with five participants including parents, individuals with mobility challenges, and flight staff.
  • Affinity Mapping: Organized feedback into recurring pain points and usability issues using FigJam.

These insights helped redesign the user flow, introducing Special Assistance earlier in the booking process for better visibility and accessibility.

Key Research Insights

  • Low awareness of what Special Assistance includes.
  • Preference for premium airlines due to better support systems.
  • Users want to avoid complications at the airport and during security checks.
  • Many rely on customer service for clarity instead of self-service options.

Design Approach

Two major user flows were designed:

  • Adding Special Assistance during the flight booking process.
  • Requesting Special Assistance before departure.

Low-fidelity wireframes were created to map the experience, including screens for:

  • Special Assistance introduction
  • Accessibility options
  • Child-related support

Usability testing was conducted with six participants (ages 25–35), and several design iterations were made to improve clarity and interaction flow.

Challenges

  • Balancing consistency with innovation while working within RyanAir’s existing design system.
  • Updating outdated UI components without losing the brand identity.
  • Ensuring accessibility while maintaining simplicity and visual balance.

Result

  • Clearer communication through informative messages after selecting Special Assistance.
  • Redundant options removed to simplify the user journey.
  • Prototype enhanced with animations and realistic loading states.

The updated design significantly improved the experience for users requiring Special Assistance, reinforcing RyanAir’s commitment to inclusivity and customer satisfaction.
